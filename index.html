Lluis Artus Suarez's website

I am a computational chemist currently searching for job in Oslo and vicinities. My scientific interests are related to carbon capture and storage, reaction optimization and machine learning. My research for almost 10 years is related to the simulation of amide, CO2 and olefines hydrogenations.

In brief

I obtained a bachelor degree in Chemistry from the UB (Universitat de Barcelona) in Barcelona, Spain. The following year I obtained a master degree in Synthesis, Catalysis and Molecular Design at ICIQ (Institute of Chemical Research of Catalonia) in Tarragona. After that I moved to Norway, where I obtained a PhD degree from Oslo University in 2021. From 2022 to 2024 I worked as a post-doctoral researcher at Mårten Ahlquist's lab in Stockholm, Sweden.

Research


My projects are focused on elucidating the reaction mechanisms of catalyzed reactions. With these studies we can pint-point the pitfalls of some catalysts and the advantages of others. That information is then used to rationaly design new faster and more specific catalysts that will degrade less. These studies are done electronically, thus reducing the cost and the risks associated with a research in a wet laboratory. Computational chemistry doesn't substitute experimental chemistry but work together in synergy, guiding each other's research to minimize false hypothesis.
